Our Methodology

Alzheimer's disease and related dementias (AD/ADRD) are irreversible, degenerative neurological disorders that progressively impair brain function, primarily affecting memory, cognition, and behavior. As the global population ages, the need for innovative care solutions becomes more pressing, particularly those that can enhance safety and reduce the caregiving burden. This study introduces a mobile health (mHealth) system featuring the SafeCircle iOS prototypeâ€”an advanced, AI-powered platform designed to support both patients and caregivers. By integrating artificial intelligence with micro-radar technology, SafeCircle aims to provide a smart and responsive environment for dementia care.

The SafeCircle system offers a comprehensive set of features tailored to the needs of individuals affected by AD/ADRD. These include secure management of patient and caregiver profiles, enabling a streamlined approach to monitoring and communication. In emergencies, the system can issue real-time alerts, providing timely assistance through one-touch SOS support. Additionally, it allows users to share live locations with trusted contacts, ensuring that help can arrive swiftly if needed. A key aspect of the system is its ability to detect and record unusual events using video, which can be invaluable for caregivers and medical professionals in assessing incidents or behavioral changes.

By blending technology with compassion, SafeCircle serves as a reliable virtual care assistant that not only enhances patient safety but also significantly alleviates caregiver stress. The incorporation of micro-radar sensors improves the accuracy of patient movement monitoring, while AI algorithms assist in recognizing patterns and anomalies in behavior. This dual-technology approach offers a scalable and responsive solution for managing dementia care, especially in home-based or assisted living settings. Ultimately, SafeCircle represents a forward-thinking approach to digital health, bridging the gap between human care and intelligent automation in the management of Alzheimer's disease and related dementias.
